Entity Relationship (ER) Diagram – Understanding Task

Objective

To understand the concept of an Entity Relationship (ER) Diagram and its importance in database design.

What is an Entity?

An entity is a real-world object or concept about which data is stored in a database.

Examples:

- Student
- Employee
- Customer
- Product

What is an Attribute?

Attributes are the properties or characteristics of an entity.

Example:
Student:

- Student_ID
- Name
- Email
- Phone Number

What is a Relationship?

A relationship describes how two or more entities are connected.

Example:
A Student enrolls in a Course.

Types of Relationships

- One-to-One (1:1)
- One-to-Many (1:M)
- Many-to-Many (M:N)

Sample ER Diagram Description

Entities:

- Student
- Course

Relationship:

- Student enrolls in Course.

Attributes:

- Student: Student_ID, Name, Email
- Course: Course_ID, Course_Name

Importance of ER Diagrams

- Helps design databases effectively.
- Reduces data redundancy.
- Improves data organization.
- Makes database development easier.

Conclusion

This task helped me understand the basic concepts of Entity Relationship Diagrams, including entities, attributes, relationships, and their role in database design.
